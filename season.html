About the Boreal Season Calendar...

Why this calendar ?

The Boreal Season Calendar is just a simple attempt to create a worldwide unified calendar that doesn't take any assumption nor doesn't consider any religion or believing. Indeed, it only considers physics.

Some rules about this calendar ?

This calendar is split into twelve months composed into four quarters, as for the Gregorian calendar. Each of the four quarters are framed by the four main solar events on Earth: the solstices and the equinoxes, as for our current seasons. As those events are purely astronomical events, the respective lengths of the quarters will differ from a year to another and nothing is done to try to linearize, as we only consider physics.

Each quarter is split into three months, all following a naming conventions for the seasons they define. E.g for Spring, the three months' names will respectively be: the rising Spring, full Spring and the setting Spring. The first and the third will always count 30 days while the second one's length will be adapted to stick to the season's length.

A year starts at a boreal Winter's beginning.
